The Challenger deep yawns in imagination, once thought to be beyond the imagined limits of knowledge. We now know where the bottom point of the ocean is, and it is beyond understanding. It yawns like the void within us, and we can sink down into it.

*'And you know the darkness beyond despair, just as intimately as you know the soaring heights. Because in this and all universes, there is balance. You can't have the one without facing the other. And sometimes you think you can take it because the joy is worth the despair, and sometimes you know you can't take it and how did you ever think you could?'[^1]*

 It is the dark night of the soul, as the Spanish mystic St. John of the Cross put it. A crisis of faith, a crisis of knowing, an awareness of how small we are. 
 
 Beyond the lacuna? Hope. A new beginning. There is life there. Without sun. Without light. Without heat save that of the earth's ruptured veins. 

[^1]: Neal Shusterman. *Challenger Deep: A Heartfelt Exploration of Teenage Mental Health*. With Brendan Shusterman (Quill Tree Books, 2015).
