The Challenger deep yawns in imagination, once thought to be beyond the imagined limits of knowledge. We now know where the bottom point of the ocean is, and it is beyond understanding.

> 'The fear of not living is a deep, abiding dread of watching your own potential decompose into irredeemable disappointment when 'should be' gets crushed by what is. Sometimes I think it would be easier to die than to face that, because 'what could have been' is much more highly regarded than 'what should have been.' Dead kids are put on pedestals, but mentally ill kids get hidden under the rug.'[^1]

[^1]: Neal Shusterman. *Challenger Deep: A Heartfelt Exploration of Teenage Mental Health*. With Brendan Shusterman (Quill Tree Books, 2015).
